Kerala

God's Own Country - Backwaters, Beaches, and Lush Landscapes

Discover Kerala

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," is a tropical paradise located on the southwestern Malabar Coast of India. Famous for its palm-lined beaches, backwaters, tea plantations, and distinctive cuisine, Kerala offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural experiences.

Kerala Backwaters

Experience the tranquil backwaters of Kerala on a traditional houseboat (kettuvallam). These interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and inlets form a labyrinthine system that runs parallel to the Arabian Sea coast. The backwaters are home to unique ecosystems and provide a glimpse into rural Kerala life.

Munnar Tea Plantations

Nestled in the Western Ghats, Munnar is known for its sprawling tea plantations, pristine valleys, and cool mountain air. Visit the tea factories to learn about the tea-making process, from plucking to packaging. The rolling hills covered in emerald-green tea bushes offer spectacular views and excellent hiking opportunities.

Eco-Tourism in Kerala

Kerala is a pioneer in responsible tourism in India. The state's eco-tourism initiatives focus on conservation, community involvement, and sustainable practices. From the Silent Valley National Park to the Periyar Tiger Reserve, Kerala offers numerous opportunities to experience its rich biodiversity while supporting conservation efforts.

Cultural Experiences

Kerala has a rich cultural heritage, with ancient art forms like Kathakali (classical dance-drama), Kalaripayattu (martial art), and Mohiniyattam (classical dance). Visitors can attend performances or even take short courses to learn these traditional art forms. The state is also known for its vibrant festivals, including Onam and Thrissur Pooram.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Kerala is from September to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for exploring the backwaters, beaches, and hill stations. The monsoon season (June to August) brings heavy rainfall but transforms the landscape into a lush green paradise, perfect for Ayurvedic treatments.
